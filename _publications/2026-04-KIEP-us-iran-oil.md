abstract: >-
  This report uses a GVAR model covering 33 economies to quantify how the U.S.-Iran war could affect oil prices and major economies under three scenarios: an early ceasefire, a prolonged conflict involving a blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, and escalation involving attacks on energy facilities. Conditional forecasts indicate that oil prices remain above their prewar level through 2027 even under an early end to hostilities, with larger and more persistent increases in the other scenarios. Because Korea depends heavily on Middle Eastern energy, the report highlights risks to import costs and the supply of naphtha and LNG. It calls for immediate supply diversification, contingency planning, and a longer-term strengthening of energy security.
